Top Energy Users at Home

01

Heating & Cooling

Air conditioners and heaters are the largest category of home energy use which uses the most energy, which makes up for third of annual bills.

~3,000 kWh/yr
02

Televisions

Modern OLED and QLED sets vary widely in consumption. Screen size, brightness, and usage hours all play a critical role.

~200–500 kWh/yr
03

Washing & Drying

Front-loader machines are more efficient, Hot cycles take up 90% more energy then Cold washes.

~500 kWh/yr
04

Cooking Appliances

Induction cooktops transfer up to 90% of energy to food, compared to just 40% for gas.

~350 kWh/yr

Tips to Reduce TV Energy Use

01

Enable Eco Mode

Most modern TVs priotizes more on brightness. Switching to a Eco or Cinema Home cuts the use of power by 20–30% which is a significant amount.

02

Use Auto Brightness

This light sensors adjut the brightness of your screen to your room, which improves viewing comfort and also saves alot of power.

03

Avoid Standby

A TV left on without any use draws 1–5W continuously,always use the power switch and switch of your tech when you are not using them .

04

Right-Size Your Screen

A 65" TV uses roughly double the energy of a 42" model, be resposible and choose a size that best suits your room viewing.

Our Mission

About Us

EnergyAnalyst is a project exploring appliance energy consumption across each Australian households.

Why Energy Matters

Australia is the country with the highest use of energy . At an average of $0.33–$0.40 per kWh, smart appliance choices has a huge impact on household budgets and carbon emissions.

This project was built to show the data in a clearer format for everyone else to understand

The Project

Built as part of COS30045 — Data Visualisation at Swinburne University of Technology. This site serves as the host platform for interactive D3.js visualisations exploring energy data across the Australian market.

The codebase is version-controlled on GitHub and deployed on Swinburne's Mercury Apache server.
